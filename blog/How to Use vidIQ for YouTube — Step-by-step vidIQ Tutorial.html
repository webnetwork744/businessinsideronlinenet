How to Use vidIQ for YouTube

A practical, SEO-first guide to using vidIQ for keyword research, tags, analytics, and growth.

Get Started FAQ

Why use vidIQ for YouTube SEO?

vidIQ helps creators find high-ROI keywords, optimize tags & titles, analyze competitors, and track performance — all in one Chrome extension and web app.

Keyword researchDiscover searchable keywords with score, search volume and competition.
Tag suggestionsAuto-suggest tags that top-performing videos use.
Competitor analysisSee what works for similar channels and replicate high-impact tactics.

Step 1 — Install vidIQ

Install the vidIQ Chrome extension from the Chrome Web Store or sign up on the vidIQ website. Connect your YouTube channel to unlock personalized insights.

Step 2 — Run Keyword Research

  1. Open the vidIQ keyword inspector.
  2. Type a seed keyword (e.g., "how to edit Shorts").
  3. Filter by search volume and competition to find a high-score keyword.

Step 3 — Optimize Title, Description, Tags

Use the target keyword in the title and first 1–2 lines of description. Add suggested tags and related keywords from vidIQ’s suggestions.

Step 4 — Analyze Competitors & Trends

Check top videos for your keyword, their tags, view velocity, and audience retention signals to shape your content.

Quick wins

  • Use vidIQ’s score to choose low-competition, decent-volume keywords.
  • Copy top-performing tags and adapt them to your niche.
  • Use the vidIQ boost & A/B testing features (if available in your plan).

Frequently asked

Is vidIQ free?

Yes — vidIQ offers a free plan with basic features. Paid plans unlock advanced keyword tools, trend alerts, and A/B testing.

Can vidIQ guarantee views?

No tool guarantees views. vidIQ guides optimization so your videos are discoverable and more likely to perform well.

On-page SEO checklist (for Google search intent)

  1. Primary keyword in <title> and H1: e.g., "How to use vidIQ for YouTube"
  2. Short meta description (120–160 chars) with keyword
  3. Use 3–8 long-tail keywords naturally in headings and body
  4. Embed a tutorial video and add transcript or timestamps
  5. Use structured data (FAQ schema) and internal links
  6. Optimize page load speed and mobile layout
