Q1: What documents do I need to buy a house or flat in Islamabad?

To buy property in Islamabad, you typically need:

  • CNIC/NICOP: Valid national identity for local or overseas Pakistanis.
  • Property Sale Agreement: Signed by buyer and seller, specifying price and terms.
  • Title Deed: Verifies ownership; must be clear of disputes.
  • No Objection Certificate (NOC): Required from housing society or relevant authorities.
  • Payment Receipts: Bank receipts for down payment and installments.
  • Mutation Document: Needed to register the property in your name at the local registry.

Leo Nine Real Estate assists in verifying all documents to ensure a smooth, legally compliant transaction.
