How to Port Forward Your Android TV

Step-by-step guide to configure port forwarding on your Android TV for better connectivity and streaming performance.

Step 1: Access Your Router Settings

Port forwarding requires access to your home network router.

  • Open a web browser on your computer or mobile device.
  • Enter your router's IP address (usually 192.168.0.1 or 192.168.1.1).
  • Log in with your router credentials.
  • Locate the “Port Forwarding” or “Virtual Server” section in settings.

Step 2: Find Android TV IP Address

  • Go to Android TV Settings → Network & Internet → Wi-Fi or Ethernet.
  • Select your connected network and note down the IP address.
  • Remember the IP to assign ports for forwarding.

Step 3: Configure Port Forwarding

  • Click “Add New Rule” or similar option in your router settings.
  • Enter the Android TV IP address in the “Internal IP” field.
  • Specify the port numbers to forward (e.g., TCP/UDP ports required by apps or streaming services).
  • Save and apply the settings on your router.

Step 4: Test Your Port Forwarding

  • Use online port checking tools to verify if the ports are open.
  • Test your Android TV apps that require remote access or specific ports.
  • Restart your router and Android TV if necessary for settings to take effect.
