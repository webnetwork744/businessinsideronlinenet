6-step roadmap to build your real estate career

  1. Get licensed & join a brokerage: Complete local licensing requirements, choose a brokerage for training and leads.
  2. Learn the basics: MLS, contracts, CMA, showing procedures, and local market knowledge.
  3. Start lead generation: Choose 2–3 channels (referrals, open houses, online ads, neighborhood pages) and commit to a weekly routine.
  4. Master sales skills: Roleplay listing presentations, objection handling, negotiation and time-to-contact routines.
  5. Choose a niche: Specialize by neighborhood, property type, or client (first-time buyers, investors) to stand out.
  6. Scale with systems: Implement a CRM, templates, automation, and hire support as volume grows.

Getting started — quick checklist

  • Complete licensing and brokerage onboarding.
  • Create a simple 30-day marketing plan (open houses, 5 neighborhood posts, 2 outreach days).
  • Practice a 10-minute listing presentation and 3 common objection scripts.
  • Set up a basic CRM and track every lead source.
  • Schedule 1 hour daily for lead follow-up and 3 hours weekly for outreach.

FAQ — quick answers

Q: How long to make a stable income?
A: Many agents see traction in 3–12 months depending on market, effort, and lead channels. Consistency and systems speed up results.
Q: Should I join a team or go solo?
A: Teams provide leads and mentorship but share commissions. Solo gives independence. Consider your learning needs and cash runway.
Q: What's the best first marketing channel?
A: Open houses + neighborhood social posts are low-cost, high-return starters. Pair with a basic Google Business Profile setup.
