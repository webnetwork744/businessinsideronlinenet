How to Switch from Everything to Wired Ethernet on PC

Step-by-step guide to switch from Wi-Fi and wireless networks to Ethernet for faster, stable internet.

Why Use Wired Ethernet on Your PC?

Switching to Ethernet ensures faster speeds, stable connections, lower latency, and more reliable internet for work, gaming, or streaming. Many users search for how to switch from Wi-Fi to Ethernet on PC or how to enable wired Ethernet on Windows to improve performance.

Step-by-Step: How to Switch to Ethernet on PC

  • Step 1: Plug in your Ethernet cable from the router/modem to your PC’s Ethernet port.
  • Step 2: Open Network & Internet Settings on Windows (right-click Wi-Fi icon → Settings).
  • Step 3: Select Ethernet from the left menu and ensure it is enabled.
  • Step 4: Disable Wi-Fi by toggling it off (this ensures PC uses Ethernet only).
  • Step 5: Verify connection: Open Command Prompt → type ping google.com → check stable response.

Common Troubleshooting

  • Update your network drivers if Ethernet is not detected.
  • Restart the router and PC to refresh connections.
  • Ensure the Ethernet cable is not damaged.
  • Check Network Priority Settings in Windows to prioritize Ethernet over Wi-Fi.
