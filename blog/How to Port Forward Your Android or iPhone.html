How to Port Forward Your Android or iPhone

Step-by-step guide to configure port forwarding on mobile devices for better connectivity and remote access.

Step 1: Access Your Router Settings

Port forwarding requires access to your home network router.

  • Open a web browser on your computer or mobile device.
  • Enter your router's IP address (commonly 192.168.0.1 or 192.168.1.1).
  • Log in using your router credentials.
  • Find the “Port Forwarding” or “Virtual Server” section.

Step 2: Find Your Device IP Address

  • Android: Settings → About Phone → Status → IP Address.
  • iPhone: Settings → Wi-Fi → Tap connected network → View IP Address.
  • Note down the IP to assign ports for forwarding.

Step 3: Configure Port Forwarding

  • Click “Add New Rule” or similar in your router settings.
  • Enter your mobile device IP in the “Internal IP” or “Device IP” field.
  • Specify the required port numbers (TCP/UDP) used by apps or remote features.
  • Save and apply the settings.

Step 4: Test Port Forwarding

  • Use online port checking tools to confirm the ports are open.
  • Test apps on Android or iPhone that require port access.
  • Restart your router and mobile device if necessary.
