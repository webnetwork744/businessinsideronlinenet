Step 1: Understand Candlestick Charts

What are Candlesticks?

Candlesticks show the opening, closing, high, and low prices of crypto within a specific time frame. Green candles indicate upward movement, while red candles show downward movement.

Basic Patterns

Common candlestick patterns include Doji, Hammer, and Engulfing – each providing signals about potential trend reversals or continuations.

Step 2: Identify Support & Resistance

Support

A price level where demand is strong enough to prevent further decline. Think of it as a “floor” for prices.

Resistance

A level where selling pressure prevents the price from rising further – acting like a “ceiling.”

Step 3: Recognize Market Trends

Uptrend

Higher highs and higher lows indicate a bullish trend. Traders often buy during pullbacks.

Downtrend

Lower highs and lower lows indicate bearish movement. Traders may look for shorting opportunities.

Step 4: Learn Chart Patterns

Head & Shoulders

A bearish reversal pattern signaling the end of an uptrend.

Triangles

Ascending and descending triangles often indicate continuation patterns for ongoing trends.

Double Top / Bottom

Patterns that indicate potential reversals in market direction.

Pro Tip: Always combine chart analysis with volume, news, and market sentiment before making trading decisions.
