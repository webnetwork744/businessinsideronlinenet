Who this is for

Founders, side-hustlers, students, and anyone planning to start a business who needs clear, actionable ways to raise startup capital.

Bootstrapping

Start with personal savings, revenue reinvestment, and minimal viable products to reduce capital needs.

Crowdfunding

Pre-sales and reward campaigns on Kickstarter/Indiegogo can validate demand and raise funds without equity.

Grants & Competitions

7-step funding action plan

Step 1: Validate demand with a one-page offer or pre-sale.
Step 2: Build a simple financial model (costs, runway, break-even).
Step 3: Prepare a 1-slide pitch + 3-slide appendix for investors.
Step 4: Target low-friction sources first (friends & family, pre-sales, microloans).
Step 5: Apply for relevant grants and startup competitions weekly.
Step 6: Reach out to local angel networks & accelerators with concise pitches.
Step 7: If pursuing VC, hit product-market-match milestones before raising larger rounds.

Funding Options (Detailed)

  • Friends & Family: Fast and flexible but document terms.
  • Microloans & Community Lenders: Small amounts with favorable terms for startups.
  • Bank Loans & SBA Programs: For more established founders with collateral or guarantees.
  • Angel Investors: Early-stage funding plus mentorship.
  • Venture Capital: For high-growth startups; expect due diligence and equity dilution.
  • Revenue-Based Financing: Payback from a percentage of revenue (non-dilutive).
  • Crowdfunding: Product pre-sales, community building, and market validation.
  • Grants & Competitions: Non-dilutive—often sector or region-specific.
  • Accelerators & Incubators: Funding, mentorship, and investor demo days.
  • Pre-sales & Early Customers: Sell first to validate and fund early operations.

FAQ

How much money do I need to start?

It depends on your business model. Build a minimal viable product and a 6–12 month runway estimate to plan funding needs.

Should I give equity to raise money?

Only if the funding accelerates growth beyond what you can achieve with non-dilutive options. Consider alternatives like pre-sales and revenue financing first.

How do I find angel investors?

Start with local angel networks, LinkedIn introductions, warm intros via founders, and startup events.
