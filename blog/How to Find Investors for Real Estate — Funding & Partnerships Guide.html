5-step investor outreach plan

Step 1: Package the opportunity — 1-page deal summary with projected returns.
Step 2: Build a targeted investor list (accredited vs. private) and segment by interest.
Step 3: Use warm introductions, local meetups, and investor clubs to start conversations.
Step 4: Send concise outreach emails with a clear call-to-action (call or site visit).

Channels to Find Real Estate Investors

  • Local real estate investor meetups: High-intent, great for JV introductions.
  • Online investor platforms: RealtyMogul, CrowdStreet, and similar marketplaces.
  • LinkedIn outreach: Build credibility with content and direct outreach to accredited investors.
  • Investor clubs & networks: Local REIAs, angel groups with property-focused members.
  • Referrals from brokers & attorneys: Professionals often know capital sources.
  • Email & content funnels: Capture interested passive investors via lead magnets and newsletters.
  • Facebook groups & forums: Niche groups for passive investors and syndicators.
  • Cold direct mail: Targeted lists for high-net-worth individuals in specific ZIPs.
  • Semi-public pitch events: Local investor pitch nights and property tours.
  • Crowdfunding campaigns: Use reward or equity crowdfunding to test investor appetite.

FAQ

Do I need to be accredited to raise investment?

Rules vary by jurisdiction. Some crowdfunding and syndication platforms allow non-accredited investors, but accredited investor rules often apply for private placements—seek legal guidance.

How much equity should I offer?

Depends on deal structure and expected returns. Use comparable deals and financial models to determine fair equity splits.

What's a typical investor timeline?

From initial contact to commitment can take weeks to months—expect due diligence and several follow-ups.
