1. Choosing the Right Wallet

Hot vs Cold Wallets

Hot wallets are connected to the internet (e.g., mobile apps, web wallets). Cold wallets are offline (hardware or paper wallets). Cold wallets offer stronger security but are less convenient for daily transactions.

2. Setting Up Your Wallet

Installation & First Steps

Download a trusted wallet from the official website or app store. Create a new wallet and securely note down your seed phrase (12-24 words). This phrase is the key to recovering your funds if your device is lost.

3. Backup & Recovery

Protecting Your Seed Phrase

Write your seed phrase on paper and store it in a safe location. Avoid digital copies (screenshots or cloud storage) as they are vulnerable to hacking.

4. Security Best Practices

Stay One Step Ahead of Hackers

  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Use a strong, unique password.
  • Keep your wallet software updated.
  • Beware of phishing emails and fake wallet apps.
Pro Tip: For long-term crypto storage, consider a hardware wallet like Ledger or Trezor. It keeps your private keys offline and safe from hackers.
