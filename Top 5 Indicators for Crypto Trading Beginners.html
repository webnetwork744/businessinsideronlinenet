This step-by-step guide explains the top 5 crypto trading indicators that every beginner must understand to analyze Bitcoin, Ethereum, and altcoin price movements.

Start Learning

1. Relative Strength Index (RSI)

What is RSI?

The RSI measures market momentum on a scale of 0 to 100. A reading above 70 suggests overbought conditions, while below 30 suggests oversold conditions.

2. Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

How MACD Works

The MACD compares short-term and long-term moving averages to identify trend changes. A bullish signal occurs when the MACD line crosses above the signal line.

3. Moving Averages (MA)

Simple & Exponential Moving Averages

Moving averages smooth out price data. The 50-day and 200-day moving averages are often used to identify long-term market trends.

4. Bollinger Bands

Market Volatility Tool

Bollinger Bands use standard deviations to measure volatility. When price touches the upper band, it may indicate overbought conditions, and the lower band signals oversold conditions.

5. Volume Analysis

Why Volume Matters

Trading volume confirms the strength of price movements. High volume during a breakout usually means a strong trend, while low volume may signal weakness.

Pro Tip: Always use multiple indicators together instead of relying on just one for accurate crypto trading decisions.
