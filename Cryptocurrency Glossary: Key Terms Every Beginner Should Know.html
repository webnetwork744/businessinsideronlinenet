Basic Cryptocurrency Terms

Bitcoin (BTC)

The first and most well-known cryptocurrency, created by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2009.

Altcoin

Any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin, such as Ethereum, Litecoin, or Ripple.

Blockchain

A decentralized digital ledger that records all cryptocurrency transactions securely.

Token

A digital asset built on an existing blockchain, often representing utility or value.

Crypto Wallets

Hot Wallet

A cryptocurrency wallet connected to the internet for quick transactions.

Cold Wallet

An offline wallet (like hardware wallets) used for secure long-term storage.

Private Key

A secret code that gives access to your crypto funds. Must be kept secure.

Public Address

A unique wallet address used to receive cryptocurrency transactions.

Trading & Investment Terms

Exchange

A platform where users can buy, sell, or trade cryptocurrencies.

Fiat

Traditional government-issued currency such as USD, EUR, or PKR.

HODL

Slang for holding cryptocurrency long-term, regardless of price fluctuations.

Whale

A crypto investor holding a large amount of cryptocurrency, influencing the market.

DeFi & NFT Terms

DeFi

Decentralized Finance – financial applications built on blockchain without intermediaries.

Smart Contract

Self-executing contracts with coded rules, built on blockchains like Ethereum.

NFT

Non-Fungible Token – unique digital assets representing art, collectibles, or game items.

DAO

Decentralized Autonomous Organization – a blockchain-based governance structure.
